Analyze these sentences. Are they well-punctuated?

Answers and explanation

After a hard day at the office, I like to relax with a large gin.

Yes. Use a comma to separate introductory words that change the order of the sentence.

The recipe needed jam, flour, sugar, fruit, eggs, ketchup, and baking powder.

Yes? Use commas to separate a list of three or more items. The comma before and + last item is called an oxford comma. It is not absolute, but personally, I like to use it.

"Look at this," he whispered.

No. Use a comma to separate a quoted statement.

Paulina, his wife of many years, had decided to go and live in Greece.

Yes. Use comma(s) to separate parenthetical words. Parenthetical words are words that express additional information about a noun that is not necessary and can be omitted.

The thief was wearing impractical high heels, so she could not run fast.​

Yes. Use comma to separate independent clauses that are connected by the conjunctions For, And, Nor, But, Or, Yet, So.

Or you can use this rule; can you change the 'so' with 'therefore' without changing the meaning of the sentence? If you can, use the comma before 'so'.
